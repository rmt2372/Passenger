Machu Picchu, Peru

Rating: 4/5

Itinerary

Stay Here: Inkaterra Machu Picchu Pueblo Hotel

Located at 2,020 meters (6,627 ft.) above sea level the hotel occupies a private five-hectare (12.3-acre) mountainside reserve, just across from the train station of Machu Picchu Pueblo. The property has 12 acres of exquisite natural beauty, where 214 bird species – such as the golden-headed quetzal and the iconic Andean cock-of-the-rock – and the world’s largest native orchid collection (372 species) have been registered.

Eat Here: Full House Peruvian Cuisine

This restaurant is a gem located in the midst of Machu Picchu. They serve delicious native Peruvian food, introducing a fusion of flavors betwen the coastal, mountains and jungle. Enjoy eating your food while overlooking the Urubamba river that is right next to you.

Go Here: Santuario Historico de Machu Picchu

While Machu Picchu is place already that you should absolutely go to, there are historical sites that needs to be visited. The Santuario Historico de Machu Picchu has been around for many many years and have many history built into them by the indigenous tribe.
