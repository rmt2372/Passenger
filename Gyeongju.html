Gyeongju, South Korea

Rating: 4/5

Itinerary

Stay Here: Hotel Gyeongju GG Tourist Hotel

Gyeongju GG Tourist Hotel is situated just a 2-minute walk from the Gyeongju Intercity Bus Terminal. The hotel is a a 15-minute walk from Gyeongju Train Station, Anapji Pond and Cheomseongdae. The property is a 22-minute drive from UNESCO World heritage-listed Bulguk Temple and Seokgulam Grotto, while Singyeongju Station is a 30-minute bus ride away. It is the perfect spot for a commutable travel!

Eat Here: Hwangseong Milmyeonok

Although milmyeon — flour noodles served either in a cold broth or spicy marinade — is most closely associated with the southeastern city of Busan, Gyeongju is also a secret hotbed for this hot-weather favorite. Different from naengmyeon, made with buckwheat noodles, milmyeon noodles are much softer. Cut them with scissors before mixing into the cold broth, and add a splash of vinegar and mustard for the ultimate cool-down.

Go Here: Bulguksa Temple

Bulguksa is located on the slopes of Mount Toham. It is a head temple of the Jogye Order of Korean Buddhism and encompasses six National treasures of South Korea, including the Dabotap and Seokgatap stone pagodas, Cheongun-gyo, and two gilt-bronze statues of Buddha.
