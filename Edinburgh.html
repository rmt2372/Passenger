Edinburgh, Scotland

Rating: 4/5

Itinerary

Stay Here: Old Waverly Hotel

The Old Waverly Hotel is located right in the heart of the city and boasts spectacular views of Princes Street Gardens and Edinburgh Castle. Among the oldest hotels in the city, the Old Waverly takes pride in traditional Scottish hosting and aesthetics.

Eat Here: The Sheep's Heid

The Sheep's Heid is a rustic traditional style pub (the oldest in Scotland!) serving crowdpleasers and comfort food. It's located close to Holyrood Park, so treat yourself to a filling meal after a long day of exploring. Or have a fun night out - in addition to delicious food and drinks, the Sheep's Heid also has an old fashioned "Skittle Alley" for bowling with friends.

Go Here: Holyrood Park and Arthur's Seat

Arthur's Seat is the highest point of an ancient extinct volcano that once stood right in Edinburgh. It's a short but challenging hike that provides panoramic views of the city - get up early and watch the sun rise over the city at the peak for an especially breathtaking view. The park also features Iron Age forts, Bronze Age agricultural terraces, medieval ruins at St Anthony's chapel, and all sorts of wildlife, for a true taste of Scotland in all its glory.
